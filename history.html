The Journey through Centuries

The Rajahnate of Sugbo

Long before the arrival of the West, Cebu (then known as Sugbo) was a prosperous trade hub founded by Sri Lumay. Merchants from China, Siam, and Arabia docked at our shores to trade silk, ceramics, and spices for local gold and wax.

1521: The Cross and the Sword

Ferdinand Magellan arrived in 1521, marking the start of Christianization. While Rajah Humabon and Hara Humamay were baptized, the era also saw the historic Battle of Mactan, where Datu Lapu-Lapu successfully defended the island against Spanish forces.

1565: The First Spanish Settlement

Under Miguel López de Legazpi, Cebu became the first Spanish settlement and the capital of the East Indies. During this time, the Basilica Minore del Santo Niño and Fort San Pedro were established, forever shaping the city's architectural soul.

War and Modernization

Following the Spanish-American War, Cebu underwent rapid urbanization. Despite the heavy destruction during World War II, the resilience of the Cebuanos led to a swift reconstruction that transformed the city into a major economic center.

The Queen City of the South

Today, Cebu City is a premier hub for IT, business process outsourcing, and tourism. In February 1937, it was officially inaugurated as a chartered city, cementing its place as the heart of the Visayas and a global gateway to the Philippines.
